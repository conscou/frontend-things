arts education

Devised Theatre of the Oppressed performance at NYU

I believe that art is a tool that is humane and impacting. It strikes us all in different ways, but no one can deny its power. I focus my studies in arts education on using art as a tool to teach about diversity, inclusivity, government, and social justice. In particular, I have conducted arts-based research on how race in America weaves into the atmosphere surrounding diversity in the arts. This specifically highlights how people of color and allies can fight for representation, narratives that cater to marginalized groups, and accessibility to opportunities (in and outside of the arts).

I value art that leaves an imprint on not only a stage, but also the world around us. I strive to create an atmosphere in which people can learn about themselves and others in an artistic process. I have sat on panels, speaking about the importance of the arts to high school students. I have also participated as an actor in theatre of the oppressed and therapeutic theatre presentations, using art as a tool to discuss power and privilege.


my educational philosophy includes the following:


  1. Politics are at the root of both art and education. Don't ignore it, and impart well-rounded, socially engaging views to students. They'll take it from there.
  2. Meet people where they are at.
  3. Art and learning cannot be present without community and multiple perspectives.
  4. Process. Process. Process. It's not about the destination; it is about the journey.
